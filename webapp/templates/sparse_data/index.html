Overview

Sparse/Noisy Data Learning in PINNs leverages physical laws to guide learning when limited or noisy experimental data is available. This approach reduces dependence on large labeled datasets by incorporating fundamental physics as a form of regularization and prior knowledge.

Key Features:

  • Physics-Guided Learning: Physical laws act as regularizers
  • Data Efficiency: Learn from limited experimental data
  • Noise Robustness: Handle noisy measurements effectively
  • Data Augmentation: Physics constraints provide additional training signals
