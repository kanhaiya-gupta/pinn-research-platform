Overview

Uncertainty Quantification in PINNs focuses on quantifying and propagating uncertainties through physical systems. This approach provides probabilistic predictions and confidence intervals, enabling better decision-making in the presence of data noise, model uncertainty, and parameter variability.

Key Features:

  • Probabilistic Predictions: Provide uncertainty estimates with predictions
  • Parameter Uncertainty: Quantify uncertainty in model parameters
  • Data Uncertainty: Handle noisy and sparse experimental data
  • Model Uncertainty: Account for model structure uncertainty

Research Applications

Risk Assessment

Quantify risks in engineering systems and provide confidence intervals for safety analysis.

  • Structural reliability
  • Safety margins
  • Failure probability

Sensitivity Analysis

Identify critical parameters and their impact on system behavior and predictions.

  • Parameter sensitivity
  • Input uncertainty
  • Model robustness

Data-Driven UQ

Quantify uncertainties in data-driven models and experimental measurements.

  • Measurement uncertainty
  • Experimental error
  • Data quality assessment

Decision Support

Provide uncertainty-aware predictions for decision-making in complex systems.

  • Optimization under uncertainty
  • Robust design
  • Policy making

Advantages of PINNs for Uncertainty Quantification

Probabilistic Framework

Natural probabilistic framework for uncertainty quantification and propagation.

Differentiable UQ

End-to-end differentiable framework enables efficient uncertainty propagation.

Physics-Informed Uncertainty

Physical constraints improve uncertainty estimates and reduce epistemic uncertainty.

Computational Efficiency

Efficient uncertainty quantification compared to traditional Monte Carlo methods.
